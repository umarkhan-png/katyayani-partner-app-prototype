Internal Product Doc v1.0 · Apr 2026

We're building for
the dukaan wala.
Not the consumer.

Every screen, every label, every tap in the Katyayani Partner App is designed around one question: does this help a semi-literate agri retailer buy faster, earn more, and trust us more? If not, it doesn't ship.

01 · The Problem We're Solving
🐌
Onboarding is slow and painful
The old app asked for everything at once — forms, documents, GST, Aadhaar — in one long flow. Drop-off was high because users didn't know how many steps were left or why each was needed. And even after completing it, verification took days with no status updates, leaving new partners on hold before they could place even one order. We rebuilt this with a step-by-step flow and instant verification so partners know exactly where they stand at every point.
📦
Homepage showed a category grid, not what to buy
The old home screen was a flat category list — Pesticides, Fertilisers, Seeds. It put the entire navigation burden on the retailer. They had to know what they wanted, find the right category, scroll 80 products, then decide. No intelligence, no seasonal context, no "you usually buy this." The homepage worked like a warehouse shelf, not a buying assistant.
💸
Margin was hidden — the one number retailers care about
Retailers don't buy for themselves — they buy to resell. Their entire decision is based on margin. The old app showed only MRP and selling price, tucked inside a product detail page. There was no bulk-tier pricing visible, no scheme benefit calculation, no "what do I actually earn per bottle?" Retailers had to do mental math on every product, every time. And a PDP that doesn't answer "will this sell and what do I earn?" is a PDP that doesn't convert.
📉
App looked basic — far behind competitor benchmarks
Partners compared the app to Agrim, AgroStar, and Nurture.farm — apps that have invested heavily in design, features, and trust. The old version had no visual personality — flat white screens, generic layout, no image-forward product cards, no trust signals. No schemes surfaced, no reorder logic, no AI tools, no community features. It felt like a form attached to a catalog. In a market where retailers have real options, "functional" is not enough — the app has to feel purposeful, modern, and built specifically for agri trade.
Our answer
Rebuild every one of these four failure points — step-by-step onboarding with instant verification, a homepage that surfaces what the retailer will buy before they search, margin on every card and every tier, and a visual standard that matches the apps retailers already compare us against.
02 · Who We're Building For
Our partners are not in metros. They are in Tier 2 and Tier 3 towns — and further in.
Katyayani's distribution reaches across both Tier 2 district headquarters and Tier 3 towns deep in agricultural belts — places like Sheopur (MP), Parbhani (Maharashtra), Sikar (Rajasthan), Navsari (Gujarat), Kaithal (Haryana), Latur (Maharashtra). The majority of our partners sit in Tier 3 and rural market towns where internet is inconsistent and smartphones are budget devices. Tier 2 partners are slightly more digitally comfortable but their core buying behaviour is the same — margin-first, restock-driven, trust-dependent.
Tier 2 District headquarters & larger towns
📍 Examples: Kaithal, Latur, Barmer, Navsari, Hoshangabad
📱 Device: Android ₹10,000–18,000, consistent 4G
🛒 Monthly purchase: ₹3–10L, broader product range
💻 Digital comfort: Has used Amazon or Flipkart at least once. Open to apps but expects things to work correctly.
🎯 Expectation: Speed, accuracy, business features. Will compare us to AgroStar or Nurture.
Tier 3 & Rural Market towns, qasbas, mandis
📍 Examples: Sheopur, Parbhani, Sikar, Nandurbar, Ratlam rural
📱 Device: Android ₹6,000–10,000, patchy 4G or 3G
🛒 Monthly purchase: ₹1–5L, seasonal concentration
💻 Digital comfort: WhatsApp only. Katyayani Partner App may be their first non-social app. No prior B2B app experience.
🎯 Expectation: Simple. Hindi. Fast. One wrong step and they give up.
What Both Tiers Share
🏪
Who they are
Owner-operated agri inputs shop. Sells pesticides, fertilisers, seeds, and crop nutrition. Serves farmers directly. Monthly stock purchase of ₹1–10L depending on tier and season. Business runs on relationships and reputation in the local farming community.
🧠
How they decide what to buy
What farmers are asking for right now. What moved off the shelf last month. What gives the best margin. Word of mouth from nearby shopkeepers. Neither tier researches online — both restock based on demand they see in the shop.
📱
Digital baseline — both tiers
WhatsApp daily. UPI payments (PhonePe/GPay). YouTube occasionally. The difference is Tier 2 has more app confidence; Tier 3 needs hand-holding through every step. Design must work for both — intuitive enough for Tier 3, efficient enough for Tier 2.
⚠️
Shared fears
Being cheated on price or quality. Getting fake or substandard product. Delivery taking too long during peak season. Apps that waste time or require too many steps. Both tiers have been let down by suppliers before — trust is earned slowly and lost fast.
What This Means for UI
✓
Design for Tier 3, delight Tier 2. If the app works smoothly for the least digitally confident user, the more confident one will find it fast and clean. Never the other way around.
✓
Design for slow internet. No heavy assets or animations that break on 3G. Every screen must load and work on a budget Android in a patchy network.
✓
Hindi + short English. Labels readable by someone with Class 8 education. No jargon. Tier 2 can handle more English; Tier 3 needs Hindi first.
✓
Large tap targets. Minimum 44px touch areas. Retailers tap fast between serving customers — every mis-tap is a moment of frustration that chips away at trust.
✓
Margin always visible. Tier 2 or Tier 3 — every retailer runs on margins. Show % on every card, every tier, every order line. No mental math needed.
✓
Trust signals everywhere. Verified badge, price match guarantee, real delivery dates. Both tiers have been let down before — earn trust visually before asking for money.
02 · UI Philosophy — 6 Core Principles
01
User-Friendly & Intuitive Onboarding
Guided onboarding with instant document verification
Onboarding is the hardest step. The retailer doesn't know us yet. Each screen guides them with clear instructions, visual examples, and a step indicator so they always know how far they are. Documents are verified instantly using Document ID APIs and AI — no waiting days for a human review. The partner gets access fast and starts ordering with confidence.
One screen, one job Guided with examples Instant AI verification
02
Decision-First Homepage
Show what they'll buy before they look
A retailer opens the app with a job to do — restock. We don't show them a category grid that requires navigation. We show curated sections: seasonal must-haves, last month's reorders, schemes expiring soon. The product they need is in the first scroll.
Section-wise, not category-wise Context before catalog
03
Margin Transparency
Profit is never hidden
The retailer is running a business. Their most important number is margin. We show it on the product card, in the price tier table, on the cart summary, and on the order confirmation. Green always means "you earn this." Never decorated, never buried.
Margin % always in green Bulk discount tiers visible
04
Trust Before Conversion
PDP earns the order before checkout asks for it
The Product Detail Page is where purchase decisions are made — not the cart. So the PDP must answer every question a retailer has before they tap "Add": What does it do? Which crop? What margin do I earn? What do others buy? What schemes apply? We load the PDP with all of this — scheme details, bulk tiers, seasonal suitability, verified product info, price match guarantee — so by the time they reach checkout, the decision is already made. No pressure, no shortcuts.
PDP = decision engine No dark patterns No hidden charges
05
Keep It Simple
Simple screens. Clear actions. No confusion.
A new retailer should never feel overwhelmed. Every screen has one primary action. Product cards show name, price, margin — and nothing else. Complexity is kept out of the main flow. A user who is confused does not tap — they close the app.
One primary action per screen Less is more
06
Structured Feature Rollout
Features in phases. Core always first.
The app has many features — but they are never mixed together on one screen. Each feature lives in its own clearly separated section so the experience never feels overloaded. Features go live in phases: only after the current phase hits its success metric do we unlock the next one. Metrics decide what stays, what improves, and what gets removed — not assumptions.
Separate sections, no clutter Phase-gated releases Metrics decide keep or remove
03 · Prototype Explorer — Hidden Interactions & Flows

These interactions are intentionally built into the prototype to demonstrate real product behaviour. They are not obvious on first look — this guide tells you where to tap and what to expect.

🪔
Diwali Festive Theme Toggle
Home Screen
The home screen hero is normally in deep emerald green. Tap the "Deliver To" address button at the top of the hero section. The entire hero gradient transforms to a Diwali palette — maroon → saffron → gold — with sparkle animations, floating diyas, and a garland. Tap again to switch back.
Tap "Deliver To" in hero Toggles on/off
⭐
App Rating Flow
Home Screen — Auto Triggered
The rate-app sheet slides up automatically — it is never triggered by a CTA. To explore each state of the rating flow, open the home screen with these URL parameters:

home.html?rate=fork → Initial prompt (Happy / Not Happy)
home.html?rate=happy → Star rating for satisfied users
home.html?rate=feedback → Text feedback for unhappy users
home.html?rate=thanks → Thank-you confirmation state
Auto-triggers on open 4 states via URL param
📌
Sticky Category Bar on Scroll
Home Screen
The hero section is tall and scroll hides it. As you scroll down past the hero, a compact sticky bar slides in from the top — showing the address, search icon, notification bell, and the category shortcut row. It freezes at the top while you browse. Scroll back to the top and it collapses back.
Scroll down on Home Bar slides in from top
🛒
ADD Button → Quantity Stepper
PDP — Cart
On the Product Detail Page, tap the + ADD button. It transforms into a − qty + stepper with a filled emerald background. The cart bar at the bottom also updates live — showing item count and total. Tap − until 0 to revert back to ADD.
Tap ADD on PDP Cart bar updates live
🔓
Distributor Price Unlock Celebration
PDP — Price Section + Progress Bar
The distributor price tier on the PDP starts locked. A gold progress bar at the bottom tracks how close the order is to unlocking it. Add 5 cases using the + ADD stepper — once you hit 5, the progress bar fills, the lock icon flips to a shield, the distributor tile activates, and a full-screen celebration triggers: shield pops in, rays burst, confetti falls, success message appears.
Add 5 cases via stepper Progress bar fills to 100% Full-screen celebration
🎉
Order Success Confetti
Order Success Screen
Complete a checkout flow or open order-success.html directly. Confetti bursts on screen load — multicolour pieces fall from top with rotation. The screen shows order confirmation, estimated delivery date, and order number. Designed to create a moment of delight at the highest-value action in the app.
Auto on order-success.html Confetti burst on load
👆
Cart Bar — Expand & Minimize
Home / Explore — Cart Bar
After adding a product, the cart bar appears at the bottom. Tap it to expand into a full cart preview showing all items. Swipe down on the expanded cart to minimize it back to the compact bar. The AI FAB (green chat button) sits just above the cart bar and always remains accessible.
Tap bar to expand Swipe down to minimize
🤖
Krishi AI FAB — Pulse Animation
Home Screen — Bottom Right
The green "Ask Anything" FAB on the home screen has a breathing glow animation — the box-shadow pulses in and out every 2.8 seconds. This is intentional: it draws attention without being disruptive. Tap it to open the AI chatbot screen (ai-chatbot.html) where you can explore the voice + text interface.
Watch the glow pulse Tap → AI Chatbot screen
