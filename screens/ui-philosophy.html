Internal Product Doc v1.0 · Apr 2026

We're building for
the dukaan wala.
Not the consumer.

Every screen, every label, every tap in the Katyayani Partner App is designed around one question: does this help a semi-literate agri retailer buy faster, earn more, and trust us more? If not, it doesn't ship.

01 · The Problem We're Solving
🐌
Onboarding is slow and painful
The old app asked for everything at once — forms, documents, GST, Aadhaar — in one long flow. Drop-off was high because users didn't know how many steps were left or why each was needed. And even after completing it, verification took days with no status visibility, leaving new partners in limbo before they could place even one order.
📦
Homepage showed a category grid, not what to buy
The old home screen was a flat category list — Pesticides, Fertilisers, Seeds. It put the entire navigation burden on the retailer. They had to know what they wanted, find the right category, scroll 80 products, then decide. No intelligence, no seasonal context, no "you usually buy this." The homepage worked like a warehouse shelf, not a buying assistant.
💸
Margin was hidden — the one number retailers care about
Retailers don't buy for themselves — they buy to resell. Their entire decision is based on margin. The old app showed only MRP and selling price, buried in a product detail page. There was no bulk-tier pricing visible, no scheme benefit calculation, no "what do I actually earn per bottle?" Retailers had to do mental math on every product, every time. And a PDP that doesn't answer "will this sell and what do I earn?" is a PDP that doesn't convert.
📉
App looked basic — far behind competitor benchmarks
Partners compared the app to Blinkit, Udaan, and DeHaat. The old version had no visual personality — flat white screens, generic fonts, no image-forward product cards, no trust signals. No schemes surface, no reorder logic, no AI tools, no community. It felt like a form attached to a catalog. In a market where retailers have options, "works" is not enough — the app has to feel premium, modern, and built specifically for agri trade.
Our answer
Rebuild every one of these four failure points — step-by-step onboarding with live verification status, a homepage that surfaces what the retailer will buy before they search, margin on every card and every tier, and a visual standard that matches the apps retailers already love and trust.
02 · Who We're Building For
Primary User — The Retailer
👨‍🌾
Ramesh, Nagpur
Runs a 400 sq ft agri inputs shop. Sells pesticides, fertilisers, seeds. Buys stock worth ₹2–5L per month. Primary phone: Android ₹8,000. Uses WhatsApp daily, Zepto occasionally.
📱
Tech comfort
Can WhatsApp, browse YouTube, do UPI payments. Not comfortable with long forms, dropdowns, or anything that looks like a "government portal".
🧠
Decision style
Buys what sells fast, what farmers are asking for, and what gives the best margin. Price is important but not the only factor — trust and reliability matter more.
⚠️
Pain points
Fears: being cheated on price, fake products, delayed delivery. Existing apps feel made for city people — confusing, lots of steps, too much English.
What This Means for UI
✓
Hindi + English both. Labels in both scripts where space allows. Never assume literacy level.
✓
One action per screen. No screen should ask for more than one decision at a time.
✓
Icons + text always together. Never icon alone. The label prevents mis-taps and builds confidence.
✓
Margin always visible. This is a business app. Show % margin on every product card, every tier, every order line.
✓
Trust signals everywhere. Verified badge, price match guarantee, Katyayani branding — these aren't decorative. They address a core fear.
03 · UI Philosophy — 6 Core Principles
01
Frictionless Onboarding
Every step earns the next one
Onboarding is the hardest sell. The retailer doesn't trust us yet. Each screen must deliver immediate value or proof before asking for the next piece of information. No long forms. No step 1 of 12. One input, one tap, done.
One screen, one job Progress always visible No surprise asks
02
Decision-First Homepage
Show what they'll buy before they look
A retailer opens the app with a job to do — restock. We don't show them a category grid that requires navigation. We show curated sections: seasonal must-haves, last month's reorders, schemes expiring soon. The product they need is in the first scroll.
Section-wise, not category-wise Context before catalog
03
Margin Transparency
Profit is never hidden
The retailer is running a business. Their most important number is margin. We show it on the product card, in the price tier table, on the cart summary, and on the order confirmation. Green always means "you earn this." Never decorated, never buried.
Margin % always in green Bulk discount tiers visible
04

Trust Before Conversion
We earn the order, not demand it
CTAs never rush. Before we ask for payment, we show verified seller badge, price match guarantee, real delivery estimates, and real product names. A retailer who trusts will reorder. One who felt pressured will churn.
No dark patterns No fake urgency No hidden charges
05
Progressive Disclosure
Simple first, depth on demand
A new retailer should never feel overwhelmed. Product card shows name, price, margin — that's it. If they want to know more, they tap. Features like bulk pricing tiers, scheme calculator, crop-disease mapping are there — but only when asked for, not pushed upfront.
Show → expand → detail Collapsible sections
06
B2B Aesthetics
Professional, not pretty
No lifestyle photography. No abstract illustrations. Real product images on white backgrounds. Density is a feature — a retailer should be able to see 3 products, their prices, and margins without scrolling. Visual energy comes from typography weight and color, not decoration.
Real product images Information-dense cards Outline icons only
04 · Key Design Decisions Explained
Onboarding Why we made onboarding step-by-step
❌ What we avoided
Long registration form: Name, shop name, GST, PAN, address, Aadhaar, license — all on one screen.
No progress indicator. No explanation of why each field is needed.
Upload all documents at once before getting any access.
VS
✓ What we built
One screen = one document. License first (most familiar to the retailer), then Aadhaar, then shop photo.
Video guidance on each screen — show them exactly what the document should look like.
Progress bar always visible. "Step 2 of 4" — the end is in sight.
Explain why each doc is needed: "To verify you're a licensed pesticide dealer."
Design insight
The biggest drop in any B2B onboarding is the document upload step. We modeled it after Rapido's driver onboarding — India's most successful gig-economy onboarding — where each document gets its own screen, its own explanation, and its own camera UI. Result: completion rate goes up because the user never feels lost mid-flow.
Homepage Sections over categories
❌ Category-first grid
Home shows: Pesticides / Fertilisers / Seeds / Fungicides / Micro-nutrients.
Retailer must know what category they want, tap in, browse 80 products, find what they need.
Zero intelligence — same experience for new user and 2-year veteran.
VS
✓ Intent-driven sections
🔥 Trending in your area — what other retailers in the same region are ordering this week.
🌱 Season must-haves — based on current crop calendar (Kharif/Rabi/Zaid).
🎁 Active schemes — buy 5 get 1, cashback offers expiring soon — shown before regular products.
🔁 Reorder — products from last 2 orders surfaced immediately.
📦 Bestsellers — consistent top-movers for newer retailers.
Design insight
A retailer's monthly stock is ~80% repeat. If we surface their reorder list before they even search, the session time drops from 5 minutes to 45 seconds. The homepage isn't a catalog — it's a restock assistant that knows your shop.
Pricing Margin in green, Off% in gray — always
The rule
Margin % — always in emerald green (#0E7A4E). This is the retailer's income. Green = money in your pocket.
Off% / Discount — always in gray (#9E9E9E). This is a relative number vs MRP — useful context, not the main message.
These two numbers are never the same color. A retailer should be able to glance at a card and instantly know: green number = what I earn.
Why not red for discount?
Consumer apps use red for discounts to create excitement. This app is B2B. Red = danger, out-of-stock, warning. We never use red for positive signals.
If everything is red and bold, nothing is an alert. We reserve red for: low stock warnings, verification failure, payment errors.
Anti-pattern No "10-minute delivery" / lightning badges
This is B2B agri. Delivery is next-day or scheduled — typically 24–72 hours. Consumer quick-commerce conventions (⚡ Express, 🟢 10 min, countdown timers) would be lies. We use EDD (Estimated Delivery Date) instead — a specific date, not a vague "express" badge. Honest delivery communication builds more trust than exciting-but-false claims. One bad delivery experience undoes 10 good ones.

The one metric above all
A partner who places their third order has crossed the habit threshold. They will keep ordering. Every design decision — homepage sections, frictionless onboarding, margin visibility, trust signals — must serve this single goal: get them to order three times.
07 · What We Do Not Do
🚫
No lifestyle photography
Farmers in fields, happy families, green meadows. This is an inventory purchasing app. Product-on-white only.
🚫
No "10-min delivery" badges
B2B agri delivery is next-day/scheduled. False urgency destroys trust permanently.
🚫
No colorful filled icons
Outline/stroke SVGs only. Colorful cartoon icons feel like a children's app — unprofessional for B2B.
🚫
No shipping all features at once
Features go live only when the previous phase's success gate is hit. Complexity before trust = churn.
🚫
No redesigning from scratch
Always build on what exists. Add, refine, improve — never nuke a tested flow to replace it with something "cleaner".
🚫
No red for discounts
Red = alert/danger in this app. Off% stays gray. Margin stays green. Color carries meaning — don't dilute it.
